Acts

British North America Act

The British North America Act gave the Government of Canada jurisdiction over “Indians and Land reserved for Indians” in Canada. This meant that it was the Government of Canada’s responsibility to take care of the “Indians”, and not that of the provinces. Under this Act’s authority, the Indian Act was created in 1876.

Indian Act

Following Confederation, the Government of Canada found itself responsible for “Indians and Land reserved for Indians”. To accomplish the goal of nation building, it was decided that legislation was required to govern “Indians”.

The Indian Act was created by combining legislation used within provinces and territories. This Act provided a legal framework that permitted the Government of Canada to control the lives of First Nations people socially, economically, politically and institutionally. It used mechanisms such as:

  • Indian status
  • access to rights
  • access to land
  • resources
  • education
  • band administration

Métis and Inuit are excluded from the Indian Act.

Since 1876, there have been multiple amendments to this Act, which have increased the imbalance of power between First Nations and the Government of Canada.
