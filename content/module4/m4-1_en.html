Treaties, Comprehensive Land Claims and Self-government Agreements

Treaty making has existed for a long time and is not an exclusively European concept. As mentioned above in the historic overview, the Dish with One Spoon is a great example of a treaty between three nations.

Treaties are negotiated agreements that define the rights, responsibilities and relationships between a First Nations, Métis or Inuit community and a federal or provincial government. They are still seen by many First Nations, Métis and Inuit as a sacred agreement that has as much relevance in today’s context as it did when it was initially signed.

Every treaty is different and reflects the concerns and desires of the particular parties at the time they were signed. No one treaty can be looked at to define what rights and entitlements exist.
