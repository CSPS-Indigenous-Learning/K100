The Uncomfortable Truth

This course looks at key historical moments of the relationship between the Government of Canada and Indigenous peoples. It presents stories that will show you a renewed perspective of what Indigenous peoples experienced in the past and how this history continues to affect individuals, families, communities, society and cultures today.

It is crucial to develop your knowledge about key historical events to help you understand the relationships, old and new, between the Government of Canada and Indigenous peoples. When we recognize the impacts of history on First Nations, Métis and Inuit, we have a better understanding of the sensitivity and respectful actions needed when collaborating together. This is the next step in developing cultural competency.

Throughout the course, you will be invited to self-reflect on some important questions. We invite you to download out this learning journal to capture your thoughts.

Note: In the next courses (K101, K102 and K103), you will learn more about each group: First Nations, Métis and Inuit.

Anticipated completion time

90 minutes

Learning objectives

After this course, you will be able to…

  • describe some of the historical events that impacted Indigenous peoples
  • explain the political relationship between the Government of Canada and Indigenous peoples
  • identify legal documents, acts and cases that set out the rights of Indigenous peoples
  • describe how Government of Canada actions and decisions have created long-term impacts on Indigenous peoples
