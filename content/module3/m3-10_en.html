Question 1 of 20

Before 1950, Indigenous people in Canada were not allowed to:

Answer: All of the above

Indigenous peoples have been subject to measures that ignored their human and civil rights, including:

  • establishing the segregated reserve system
  • removing Indigenous children from their families and communities
  • forcing Indigenous children to attend residential schools
  • relocating Indigenous people from one community to another
  • imposing definitions of identity
Indian Residential Schools and Indian hospitals were the main ways that Indigenous people were controlled.

Answer: False

Indian Residential Schools and Indian hospitals were only two ways that Indigenous peoples were colonized and controlled. Other ways included the Indian Act, taking away their traditional lands and the ongoing assault on Indigenous culture. All of these factors have an impact.
