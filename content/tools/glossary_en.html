Glossary

  • Culture

    Culture can be defined as the shared values, norms, family styles, social roles and behaviours of a group of people. The people may have developed these common traits in response to the political, economic, environmental and social realities they face. Culture also refers to the things they share based on common ethnicity, race and/or nationhood.

  • Race

    Race refers to a group of people of common ancestry, who are socially defined as being different from other groups by physical traits such as colour of skin, shape of eyes, hair texture or facial features.

  • Ethnicity

    Ethnicity is made up of the many beliefs, behaviours and traditions that hold a group together. These include a common language, history, geographical location and religion. Ethnic diversity is the presence of various ethnic groups within a society or nation.

  • Cultural awareness

    Cultural awareness is understanding the cultural differences between groups, while not assigning a value to those differences.

  • Cultural sensitivity

    Cultural sensitivity is being aware of how one's own experiences and values may impact others. It also means recognizing that different experiences are valid.

  • Cultural competency

    Cultural competency is an ongoing learning process that gives you the ability to work effectively with people of different cultures or socio-economic backgrounds.

  • Cultural humility

    Cultural humility is being able to see your own culture in relation to other cultures without a sense of superiority

  • Cultural genocide

    Cultural genocide is the destruction of those structures and practices that allow the group to continue as a group. States that engage in cultural genocide set out to destroy the political and social institutions of the targeted group. Land is seized, and populations are forcibly transferred and their movement is restricted. Languages are banned. Spiritual leaders are persecuted, spiritual practices are forbidden, and objects of spiritual value are confiscated and destroyed. And, most significantly to the issue at hand, families are disrupted to prevent the transmission of cultural values and identity from one generation to the next.

  • Cultural safety

    Cultural safety focuses on responsive services. Its goal is to create equitable health and socio-economic outcomes for Indigenous people.

  • Duty to consult and accommodate

    Federal and provincial governments have a legal duty to consult and, if appropriate, to accommodate when the Crown has knowledge of the potential existence of Indigenous rights or title and considered behaviours that might adversely affect Indigenous communities and peoples.

  • Intergenerational Trauma

    It is the legacy of numerous traumatic events a group of people experiences over generations and encompasses the psychological and social responses to such events. Source: University of Calgary, Aboriginal Intergenerational Trauma

  • Two spirit

    “Two spirit” refers to a person who has both masculine and feminine spirits. It is used by some First Nations people to describe their sexuality, gender or spiritual identity.

  • Department of Indian Affairs

    The Department of Indian Affairs was initially created to manage military alliances. Over time, it evolved to manage not only Canada's relationship with First Nations, Inuit and Métis.

  • Non-Insured Health Benefits

    The Non-Insured Health Benefits (NIHB) Program is a national program that provides coverage to registered First Nations and recognized Inuit for a specified range of medically necessary items and services that are not covered by other plans and programs.

  • Sanatorium

    A sanatorium is a medical establishment for long-term illness, most typically associated to tuberculosis (TB) before the discovery of antibiotics. They were used to avoid the spreading of diseases.

  • Crowded

    Crowded is defined as a household that contains more than 1.0 person per room.
