Principles respecting the Government of Canada's relationship with Indigenous peoples

As you have seen, the relationship between the Government of Canada and First Nations, Métis and Inuit has a complex history. It is likely not what you were taught in school.

The Government of Canada has decided that our laws and policies need to be reviewed:

  • to recognize the unique place that Indigenous peoples have in the Canadian political and constitutional framework
  • to be inclusive as set out in the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples

The 10 Principles respecting the Government of Canada’s relationship with Indigenous peoples have been developed to help public service employees build a relationship with Indigenous peoples based on partnership and recognition of rights.

As public service employees, we are being asked:

  • to take a broader perspective
  • to reflect on our blind spots
  • to do our part in renewing and advancing the relationship with Indigenous peoples
