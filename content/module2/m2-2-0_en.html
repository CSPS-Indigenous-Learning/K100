Constitution Act, 1982

Section 35

In 1982, section 35 was added to the Constitution Act. This section recognized, affirmed and protected the existence of the following collective rights:

Treaty rights

A treaty is a solemn agreement between the Crown and an Indigenous group that sets out promises, obligations and benefits for both parties. Section 35.1 of the Constitution Act, 1982 defines treaty rights as including “rights that now exist by way of land claims agreements or may be so acquired.”

Aboriginal rights

These are rights around a practice, custom or tradition that was part of a distinctive Indigenous culture before European contact. It includes the use and management of lands and natural resources - for example, for fishing, hunting and harvesting. It also includes the rights to cultural protection and meaningful implementation of treaties.

For the Métis, this means a practice, custom or tradition that was an integral part of the distinctive culture of the historic Métis community at the date of effective European control.

Aboriginal title

Aboriginal title recognizes an Indigenous group’s collective interest in a specific territory based on long-standing use and occupancy of the land. That said, the Government of Canada and other third parties should be collaborating and consulting with Indigenous communities prior to making decisions on the use of land.

The Constitution Act, 1982 does not define the full set of Aboriginal rights. These rights are continuously being clarified and articulated through historic and Modern Treaties, self-government agreements and court cases.


Constitution Act, 1982 – section 35

Recognition of existing aboriginal and treaty rights

35. (1) The existing aboriginal and treaty rights of the aboriginal peoples of Canada are hereby recognized and affirmed.

(2) In this Act, “aboriginal peoples of Canada” includes the Indian, Inuit and Métis peoples of Canada.

(3) For greater certainty, in subsection (1) “treaty rights” includes rights that now exist by way of land claims agreements or may be so acquired.

(4) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Act, the aboriginal and treaty rights referred to in subsection (1) are guaranteed equally to male and female persons.

35.1 The Government of Canada and the provincial governments are committed to the principle that, before any amendment is made to Class 24 of section 91 of the “Constitution Act, 1867”, to section 25 of this Act or to this Part,

(a) a constitutional conference that includes in its agenda an item relating to the proposed amendment, composed of the Prime Minister of Canada and the first ministers of the provinces, will be convened by the Prime Minister of Canada; and

(b) the Prime Minister of Canada will invite representatives of the aboriginal peoples of Canada to participate in the discussions on that item.
